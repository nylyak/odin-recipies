Cranberry Pear Pie

This cranberry pear crumble pie is a new favorite this time of year. Between those creamy pears, the bursts of tart cranberry, the flaky pie crust, and the buttery brown sugar crumbles—there isn’t a soul on Thanksgiving Day who won’t beg you for this pie recipe. Guaranteed.

Have I mentioned it’s juicy? All the normal pie squad is here including sugar to sweeten, flour to thicken, cinnamon and ginger for extra yum, and a little squeeze of lemon to keep the flavors bright. Fresh or frozen cranberries work wonders for a ruby red pop of flavor and our FAVORITE pears bring it all home.

Ingredients

  • 1 recipie of flaky pie crust (see Odin Recipies)
  • 12 oz. of fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup of mapel syrup
  • 6 large pears
  • 4 1/2 teaspoons of arrowroot disolved in 2 tablespoons of water

Steps

  1. Line a 9 in. pie plate with flaky pie crust dough and reserve the rest for making lattice
  2. Place cranberries and maaple syrup in a saucepaan
  3. Peel and core the pears and cut into 1/2 in. pieces, adding to maple syrup as you cut
  4. Bring syrup to a boil and cook, stirring, for several minutes until cranberries begin to pop
  5. Add the arrowroot mixture aand cook another minute more, stirring constantly
  6. Let cool slightly
  7. Pour into pie shell.
  8. Make a lattice to cover the pear mixture and paake at 350 degrees for about 45 minutes
